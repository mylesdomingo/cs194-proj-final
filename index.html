Overview

In this project, we use a defined dataset of images and perform simple operations like shifting and averaging to reproduce complex camera effects.

Depth Refocusing

Shifting images in our dataset by a specific amount and averaging them allows us to recreate an image at different focus depths. Using the coordinates of our grid (17x17), we center our images around the center image and shift it by their respective assigned coordinates in grid space. This shift, denoted by alpha, is scaled to achieve different depths. Here, we shift our image sets using an alpha range between (-3, 3) in 30 timesteps.

Here are selected individual frames, where the focus is on front, mid, and back sections of the image, respectively.

Aperture Adjustment

We can vary the amount of photos in our averaging operation to adjust aperture affects. We note a heuristic r, which tracks how many images from our center image in our (17x17) grid to take. For example, r=0 takes on the center image. For r=70, we average 141 images. To adjust aperture, we vary r while focusing on a specific point on our image. The gifs show a 30 timestep aperture adjustment for r between (0, 144).
